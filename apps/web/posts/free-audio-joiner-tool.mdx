## Why In-Browser Processing Matters

Uploading audio to a third-party server means handing over your recordings — interview raw cuts, music drafts, voice notes — to hardware you don't control. Most services store those files longer than you'd expect.

This tool runs [FFmpeg](https://ffmpeg.org) via WebAssembly, directly in your browser. No file ever leaves your device. Once the tool loads, it works without an internet connection. Your audio stays yours.

## How It Works

1. **Drop your files.** Drag and drop one or more audio files, or click to browse. Each file appears as a waveform row.
2. **Trim each clip.** Drag the start and end handles on any waveform to cut the portion you want to keep. Everything outside the handles is discarded.
3. **Reorder the segments.** Use the up and down arrows on each row to arrange clips in the order they should play.
4. **Preview before you commit.** Hit play to hear the full joined sequence. Click anywhere on a waveform to jump to that point. Spacebar toggles play and pause.
5. **Choose a format.** Pick MP3, WAV, or M4A as your output.
6. **Join and download.** One click processes all segments and triggers a download.

No account required.

## Supported Output Formats

- **M4A** — efficient compression, native to Apple devices
